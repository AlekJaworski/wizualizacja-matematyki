An *event* is a place AND a time. Four numbers, not three. "Warsaw, central station, 18:00 on Friday" picks out a single event. Move any one of the four — the time, the city, the building, the platform — and you're at a different event.<br><br>This is the seed of *spacetime*. Time is not a separate thing that flows past while the universe sits still. It's a coordinate, packaged together with the spatial coordinates.<br><br>Special relativity will turn out to put time on *almost* the same footing as space — there's a sign difference in how distances are measured (see [[minkowski_metric]]) and that sign is the entire content of SR. But the move from "space, plus time as a separate parameter" to "spacetime as a four-dimensional thing" happens *here*, before any relativity. It's a conceptual reframe.<br><br>Once events are points in spacetime, *trajectories* of objects become curves through spacetime — every moment of your life is a point, the whole life is a curve called your *worldline*. Light travels at 45° (in appropriate units) on a spacetime diagram. This is the language all of relativity is written in.

<!-- example -->
A friend texts: "meet at the cafe". You text back: "when?". You both know the cafe (3 spatial coordinates: address). Without a time, the meeting doesn't pick out an event — it picks out a *worldline* of the cafe through time. The text "Friday at 6 PM" supplies the fourth coordinate and reduces the worldline to a single event. Specifying an event takes 4 numbers.

<!-- mistakes -->
- **"Time is what flows past while space stays still"** — pre-relativistic intuition. Useful for everyday life, useless for thinking about GR or even SR. Replace with: time is one of four coordinates labeling events.
- **"Time and space are the same thing in spacetime"** — almost, but not quite. The Minkowski metric has a sign that makes time *different from* space in a precise way. Lumping them together is an over-correction.
- **"Worldlines are just trajectories"** — close. Trajectories live in space and are parameterized by time. Worldlines live in spacetime and *include* the time coordinate. Same physical content, different framing.
